Sustain Our Soil

Human centered design highlight: Develop


Data product build and deployment




The Sustain our Soil for our Food interactive web application was a visual appeal to food consumers to engage in their food chain and ecosystem role at the intersection of soil health and their food. This app was designed and developed by DADEDA Principal Kathryn Hurchla as her graduate degree capstone project with Maryland Institute College of Art's Data Analytics & Visualization MPS program in 2021. The app was available publicly through 2022 on a standalone domain accessible from this DADEDA website and has also been promoted through the school's website mica.edu/offices-divisions/office-of-open-studies/mps-programs along with other works of her graduate program cohort.

Made in the open source Python programming language with Plotly Dash, Flask, and with supportive libraries, Kathryn optimized it for responsiveness and scalability with users on desktop web and mobile devices. She ran the app on the Heroku platform and deployed it using Git and the Heroku command line interface (CLI).

Impact focused


With 7.9 Billion humans on Earth as of November 2021, and an expected 10 billion in the year 2057 (United Nations/Worldometer), we hold vast potential to sustain and restore the grand resource of our soil through the simple act of eating. We have the purchasing power to support food producers, and advocacy to reach policymakers. Around such a humble routine, low effort shifts in behavior determine and drive food industry change.

This project investigated the intersection of organic carbon storage in soil with climate change and food production sustainability. The intent of the web-based interactive data visualization application was to spark curiosity and compel consumers to engage in their food chain and ecosystem role, by exploring the countries some of their food comes from and the organic carbon content in soils as a metric of health that extends beyond soil to us and to the planet.
